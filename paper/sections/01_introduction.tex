\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}


The literature on Market Regimes is large and widely discussed in academia. What's especially interesting, is the connection between the Market Regime literature and findings in empirical finance on international market correlations. 
For example, it is a described phenomenon, that stock market volatility is higher when markets go down. Additionally, it is also well known that in periods of high volatility, stock markets become more correlated and in periods of low volatility, they are less correlated.
As an exercise in this paper, the motivation is to implement a \gls{hmm} to infer market regimes from real financial data, by using the Python programming language. 
The research question this works intends to answer, is whether an HMM captures periods of elevated international equity market correlation.
